# CompText AIR Lexicon

This document defines key terms and concepts related to the CompText Agent Intermediate Representation (AIR).

## Terms and Definitions

*   **AIR Artifact**: A discrete, immutable, and verifiable JSON object representing an agent's action, proposal, or a step in its operational flow. It is the fundamental unit of communication and audit in the CompText ecosystem.
*   **Evidence Record**: A verifiable JSON object that captures the outcome and context of executing a command or action associated with an AIR Artifact. It provides cryptographic proof of execution.
*   **Agent ID**: A unique identifier assigned to a specific agent instance. Used to trace the origin of AIR artifacts and actions.
*   **Task ID**: A unique identifier for a larger, overarching task or operational session that an agent is engaged in. It allows for grouping of related AIR artifacts and evidence records.
*   **Proposal**: The intended action or output generated by an agent, encapsulated within the `payload` of an AIR Artifact.
*   **Payload**: The generic JSON object within an AIR Artifact that contains the specific details of an agent's proposal, action, or intermediate data. Its structure is dynamic and depends on the agent's context.
*   **Hash Chain**: A sequence of cryptographically linked AIR artifacts, where the `input_prompt_hash` of a subsequent artifact refers to the `output_proposal_hash` of a preceding one. This mechanism ensures end-to-end verifiability and auditability of an agent's operational history.
